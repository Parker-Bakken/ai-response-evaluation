# Example 01 — Explain black holes to a 10-year-old

## Prompt
Explain black holes to a 10-year-old.

## Candidate Response A
A black hole is like a super-strong vacuum in space. It’s made when a huge star collapses and squeezes into a tiny spot.
The gravity is so strong that even light can’t escape, which is why we can’t see it directly.
Scientists “see” black holes by watching how nearby stars and gas move, and by detecting ripples in space called gravitational waves.

## Candidate Response B
A black hole forms when a massive star collapses beyond the Schwarzschild radius, creating an event horizon. The singularity is a region where spacetime curvature diverges. Hawking radiation can occur due to quantum effects near the horizon.
